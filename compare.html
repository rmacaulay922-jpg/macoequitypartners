Where the national tools win

Breadth, features, maturity

They cover every US county; Maco never will. They also bundle more workflow than Maco does — mobile apps, built-in skip tracing and marketing tools on various plans. Maco gives you ranked county-record leads, signals, underwriting, letters and exports, and expects you to run outreach with your own stack.

Where Maco wins

Depth in six Florida counties

Maco reads the county’s own records instead of a resold national layer: Miami-Dade live code cases and recorded code liens, homestead status from the county roll, owner mailing addresses from the state roll, and ZIP sale bands built from recorded arm’s-length sales — 92 ZIPs across Broward, Lee, Collier, Polk and the Clermont area, built 2026-07-18. And where a county publishes nothing, the product says so instead of estimating.

Fair questions

Asked before switching

Is Maco a full PropStream replacement?

Only if your buy box sits inside the six covered counties. If you work deals in multiple states, you would be giving up national coverage that Maco does not have and will not pretend to have. Inside Miami-Dade, Broward, Lee, Collier, Polk and Lake, Maco replaces list-building with leads ranked from the county’s own records — and in Miami-Dade adds live code and lien signals the national tools don’t carry.

Why is Maco $50 when they charge $99–$119?

Smaller scope, launch pricing. Six counties and one product cost less to run than a national data license, and $50/month is the founding rate (July 2026) — it holds for as long as your subscription stays continuously active. Standard access is $75/month.

Do I need a credit card for the trial?

No. The 7-day trial takes an email, not a card, and there is no auto-bill at the end. It becomes a paid subscription only if you decide it should.

Does Maco do skip tracing, dialers or direct-mail campaigns?

No. Maco gives you the owner’s mailing address from the government roll (with out-of-state and absentee flags), mail-merge letters, and CSV export. Bring your own skip-tracing and outreach tools — many investors already pay for those separately anyway.

Is the data depth the same in all six counties?

No, and we label the difference instead of hiding it. Miami-Dade is the deepest market: live comps, per-property ARV, open code cases, recorded liens and permit signals. Broward, Lee, Collier and Polk run on county tax-roll leads with owner mailing, sqft and year built, and ZIP qualified-sale bands — no per-property ARV. Lake is the smallest: 120 Clermont-area leads with the thinnest fields. The markets page keeps the honest tier list.
