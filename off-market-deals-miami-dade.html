Comps, not models

ARV you can argue with

Every Miami-Dade ARV is built from recorded county sales near the property — you see the comp count, the $/sf basis, a low/high range and a confidence label. If the comps are thin, the product says “needs comps” instead of faking precision.

Signals that mean something

Code cases and liens move deals

An open code case or a recorded county lien is a cost, a deadline and a motivation signal all at once. Maco correlates them to the scored properties by address, flags county-flagged properties that aren’t in the lead set yet, and folds lien payoffs into the underwriting so the profit math stays honest.
