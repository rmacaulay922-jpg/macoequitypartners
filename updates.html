What's New

Dated. Specific. Shipping constantly.

Every entry below is a real change that went live on the date shown. No roadmap promises — only what shipped.

July 19, 2026

Equity flags, automatic listing checks, and a faster portal

  • Modeled equity chips on Miami and Polk leads — HIGH (~70%+) and LOW (<30%) equity flags from recorded sale history, clearly labeled as modeled estimates. Chase the free-and-clear estates; skip the underwater ones.
  • Automatic MLS listing check — bring a free RentCast key and the letter picker flags LISTED properties (price + days on market) before you spend postage. No key? Manual check links on every row.
  • Sort & filters on every county's lead board — sort by county value, sale band, years held, or sqft; one click hides Wall Street portfolio owners (244 in Broward alone) or shows only band-priced leads.
  • Crime chips rebuilt from FDLE agency data — every city PD in all six counties, banded on violent crime. A chip only shows when the city's own department reports its own numbers.
  • Portal loads faster — data files no longer block first paint.
  • Fixed: listing links now include the ZIP (Homestead-area links previously resolved to the wrong city), Lake County letters un-blocked (119 of 120 mailable), land-only parcels no longer show house-price bands.
July 18, 2026

Sale bands, mailing addresses, and honest letters

  • ZIP sale bands for Broward, Lee, Collier, Polk and Lake — a price range per ZIP built from 30,721 recorded arm's-length sales (shown as a range, never called an appraisal).
  • Sqft & year built joined onto all 2,666 non-Miami leads from the state tax roll.
  • Owner mailing streets baked for 2,394 of 2,397 Miami properties — letters no longer depend on a live county lookup.
  • Tiered letters — Miami letters can quote a real computed offer (only on comp-verified deals with a scoped repair); band counties quote the recorded-sales range; everywhere else, no number at all.
  • Your letters, your name — subscribers set their own name and phone once; letters and letterhead merge with your identity.
  • FEMA flood zone on lead detail — live from the National Flood Hazard Layer, with the insurance implication spelled out.
July 17, 2026

Refresh dates you can see

  • Every county banner now shows its refresh date — the quiet proof the data is moving.
  • The nightly refresh pipeline was rebuilt to survive the state data host's rate limits (its cooldowns are longer than most tools' patience — ours now waits them out).
  • Prospect tooling, week-one playbook, and the marketing kit shipped alongside.
