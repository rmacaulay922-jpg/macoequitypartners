Signal depth

What the Lake data includes — and what it doesn’t.

Lake is the thinnest county we cover, and the product says so on its own banner. It is in the lineup for one reason: the Clermont corridor is a real acquisition market, and these 120 owners are real. Expect targeting data, not analysis data.

In the product today

  • 120 non-homestead single-family leads in the Clermont / south Lake area — pulled 2026-07-11 from the Lake County Property Appraiser GIS
  • Owner-type tags: 94 life estates, 26 trusts — the two structures that most often precede an estate sale
  • Owner mailing address for every lead — 31 mail out of state, 63 more are in-state absentee
  • Sale history from the county record: hold length on every card — 44 leads held 20+ years, 9 held 30+
  • County just/market, assessed and taxable values, building and land split
  • Clermont-area market context from three published ZIP sale bands (34711, 34714, 34715)
  • Mail-merge letters and CSV export, same as every county

Not here — on purpose

  • No per-property ARV. Lake publishes no comp or code-violation feed, and we won’t print a number a feed can’t back
  • No live code-violation or lien feed
  • No per-lead sqft / year-built join yet — the county’s roll extract also omits property ZIPs, so leads aren’t matched to sale bands individually. The three Clermont-area bands are market context, not per-property pricing
  • This is the smallest list we publish. If you need volume, start with Polk or Broward; if you farm Clermont specifically, this list earns its keep
